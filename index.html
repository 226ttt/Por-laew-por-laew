1. Introduction

What is a Pneumatic Cylinder?

A pneumatic cylinder is a mechanical device that converts the energy of compressed air into linear motion. It drives a piston inside a barrel using pressure differences on both sides.

Types of Pneumatic Cylinders

  • Single Acting: Uses air pressure for one direction, spring returns it.
  • Double Acting: Uses air for both extension and retraction (most common).
  • Rodless Cylinder: No piston rod extends out, saves space.
  • Rotary Actuator: Provides rotational motion.

Applications

Widely used in automated production lines such as automotive, packaging, electronics, food industry, and workpiece clamping systems.

2.1 3D Design

Creating 3D models helps verify assembly and simulate operations before actual manufacturing. Software like SolidWorks, Inventor, or CATIA can be used.

2.2 CAT3D (CATIA V5 3D Modeling)

Using CATIA V5 to create Parts and Assemblies for pneumatic cylinders.

  • Part Design: Create main components (Barrel, Piston, Rod)
  • Assembly Design: Assemble components together
  • DMU Kinematics: Check motion and interference

2.3 CAT2D (Engineering Drawing)

Extracting 2D drawings from 3D models for manufacturing, showing dimensions, tolerances, and surface finish symbols.

  • Show Orthographic Projection & Section Views
  • Specify GD&T for CNC processes
  • Export as .PDF or .DXF for production

2.5 Materials

  • Aluminum 6061-T6: Lightweight, corrosion resistant. Ideal for Barrel and End Caps.
  • Stainless Steel 304: High strength, rust-proof. Used for Piston Rod.
  • NBR Rubber: Good elasticity, resistant to oil and compressed air. Used for seals.
  • PTFE: Low friction coefficient, high heat resistance.
